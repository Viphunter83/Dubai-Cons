DUBAI MUNICIPALITY BUILDING CODE - KEY REGULATIONS SUMMARY (MOCK DATA)

1. VILLA REGULATIONS
- Minimum ceiling height for habitable rooms is 3.0 meters (9.84 feet).
- Minimum ceiling height for service rooms (bathrooms, kitchens) is 2.4 meters.
- Maximum building height for private villas (G+1) is 12 meters from ground level.
- Setback from street: Minimum 3.0 meters.
- Setback from neighbors: Minimum 3.0 meters on all sides.
- Boundary wall height: Maximum 2.0 meters.
- Privacy guidelines: Windows overlooking neighbors must be obscured or placed at high levels (min 1.8m sill height).

2. PALM JUMEIRAH SPECIFIC REGULATIONS (NAKHEEL)
- Architecture style must follow specific frond themes (Mediterranean, Arabic, Contemporary).
- Color palette: Earthy tones, white, beige. Bright or neon colors are strictly prohibited.
- Roof type: Pitched roofs required for specific themes; flat roofs allowed for modern styles with specific approval.
- Swimming pools: Must be within property boundary, min 1.5m from boundary wall.
- Beach access: Unobstructed access required; no permanent structures in the beach setback zone.

3. APARTMENT/COMMERCIAL REGULATIONS
- Corridors minimum width: 1.5 meters for residential, 1.8 meters for commercial.
- Fire safety: Sprinklers mandatory in all rooms. Smoke detectors required.
- Accessibility: Ramps required for all level changes > 6mm. Ramp slope max 1:12.
- Minimum door width: 0.9m for entrances, 1.0m for accessible units.

4. SUSTAINABILITY (AL SA'FAT)
- Windows: Must be double-glazed with thermal break.
- Water efficiency: Low-flow fixtures required.
- Lighting: LED lighting mandatory for at least 75% of connected load.
- Solar: Solar water heaters mandatory for all new villas.

5. GENERAL INTERIOR DESIGN
- Kitchen ventilation: Hoods must extract to outside.
- Bathroom ventilation: Mechanical extraction required if no window.
- Balcony safety: Railing height minimum 1.1 meters. Gaps max 10cm.
